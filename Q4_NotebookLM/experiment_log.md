# Q4 NotebookLM Experiment Log

## Assessment Context

This experiment tested Google NotebookLM as an AI-assisted research tool for analysing ESG reporting standards. The notebook was used to upload ESG reporting sources, ask source-grounded questions, generate summaries, create an audio overview, and compare information across multiple documents.

## Notebook Details

- Notebook name: ESG Reporting Standards Analysis
- Organisation context: GreenCorp Australia
- Main topic: ESG reporting standards and sustainability disclosure frameworks
- Main source uploaded: GRI Universal Standards 2021 FAQ
- Additional sources: ESG / sustainability reporting framework documents uploaded to the same notebook

## Feature 1: Source-Grounded Q&A

Question asked:

```text
What are the key principles of ESG reporting?
```

Purpose:

This tested whether NotebookLM could answer a direct ESG reporting question using the uploaded sources and provide citations back to the source documents.

Evidence screenshot:

```text
notebooklm_qa_screenshot.png
```

Observation:

NotebookLM provided a structured response explaining ESG reporting principles and displayed citation bubbles linked to the uploaded sources. This was useful because it allowed the answer to be checked against the original materials.

## Feature 4: Notebook Guide / Reports

Action completed:

The Notebook Guide / Reports feature was used to generate a structured learning or study-style output from the uploaded ESG sources.

Evidence screenshot:

```text
notebooklm_guide_screenshot.png
```

Observation:

The generated guide helped organise complex ESG reporting information into a more readable structure. This can support report planning and revision, but it should be edited and checked before academic use.

## Feature 5: Cross-Document Synthesis

Question asked:

```text
What are the main differences between the ESG reporting frameworks in my sources?
```

Evidence screenshot:

```text
notebooklm_synthesis_screenshot.png
```

Observation:

NotebookLM compared ideas across multiple uploaded sources. This was useful for understanding how different ESG reporting frameworks vary in purpose, audience, and reporting focus.

## Overall Evaluation

NotebookLM was useful for ESG research because it supported source-grounded Q&A, document summarisation, audio-based learning, guided report generation, and cross-document comparison. The tool improved efficiency and helped simplify complex sustainability reporting material.

However, it should not be used as a standalone authority. The quality of the output depends on the quality and completeness of the uploaded sources. Important claims should be checked against the original documents, especially where reporting obligations, compliance, or formal ESG disclosures are involved.
